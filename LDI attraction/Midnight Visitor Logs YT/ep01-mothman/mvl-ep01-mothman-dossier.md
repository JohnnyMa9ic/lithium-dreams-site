## ▌ INCIDENT LOG

The following entries represent the documented sequence of events constituting the Point Pleasant anomaly period. All dates, locations, and attributions are drawn from contemporary news sources, law enforcement records, and the field investigations of journalist John A. Keel (1966–1967).

---

**ENTRY 001**
`▸ DATE: August 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Jefferson Avenue, Point Pleasant, West Virginia`
`▸ TYPE: Interior Apparition`

A Point Pleasant resident identified in contemporaneous records as Lawrence Gray reported the appearance of a six-foot figure inside his home. Description: dirty gray and white coloration, described by the witness as "almost lunar." Witness reported full-body paralysis lasting the duration of the encounter. Duration: under one minute. No witnesses beyond primary observer. This incident was not publicly reported at the time of occurrence and entered the record only after the subsequent November events prompted broader community disclosure.

---

**ENTRY 002**
`▸ DATE: November 1, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Camp Conley Road Armory, Point Pleasant area`
`▸ TYPE: Perimeter Sighting`

A National Guardsman assigned to the armory reported observing a large brown humanoid figure perched in a tree near the facility perimeter. No official report was filed at time of incident. The account entered the record retroactively. Witness chose not to be identified.

---

**ENTRY 003**
`▸ DATE: November 12, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Cemetery, Clendenin, West Virginia`
`▸ TYPE: Aerial · Daylight`

Five gravediggers at work in a Clendenin cemetery observed a brown, manlike figure with wings emerge from adjacent tree line and glide low over their position. Primary witness: Kenneth Duncan. Partial corroboration: additional workers present, though not all confirmed the sighting. Duration of visibility: seconds. The entity did not land or interact with the witnesses.

---

**ENTRY 004**
`▸ DATE: November 14, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Salem, West Virginia (approx. 90 miles northeast of Point Pleasant)`
`▸ TYPE: Indirect — Electronic Interference + Animal Disappearance`

Witness Merle Partridge reported anomalous television interference (patterned, non-random) accompanied by a high-pitched auditory emission from the set. Witness's German Shepherd (name: Bandit) oriented toward the barn and began vocalizing. Flashlight sweep of property revealed two red lights described as "circular, close together — reflecting back like bicycle reflectors." The animal ran toward the light source. The animal did not return. No physical evidence recovered.

*This entry is classified as indirect because the primary anomalous entity was not observed directly. The secondary evidence (animal response, disappearance) is preserved in the record as potentially significant.*

---

**ENTRY 005 — PRIMARY INCIDENT**
`▸ DATE: November 15, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Abandoned North Power Plant, TNT Area, Mason County, WV`
`▸ TYPE: Vehicle Pursuit · Multiple Witnesses · Police Report Filed`

Four witnesses in a single vehicle. Two couples: Roger Scarberry, Linda Scarberry, Steve Mallette, Mary Mallette. Approximate time: late evening.

Witnesses observed a large gray figure with wings folded against its body standing near the road adjacent to the North Power Plant ruins. Eyes described as large, circular, red — appearing to glow in vehicle headlights. Witnesses reversed direction and accelerated toward Point Pleasant on Route 62. Entity spread wings (estimated span: 10 feet or greater) and pursued the vehicle.

According to Roger Scarberry: the entity maintained pace with the vehicle at speeds exceeding 100 mph without apparent wing movement (gliding only).

**Report status: Filed with Mason County Sheriff's department the evening of November 15, 1966.** The Point Pleasant Register published the account on November 16, 1966. A sub-editor designated the entity "Moth Man" — the label was a reference to the then-popular Batman television series and was not intended as a formal designation. It became one.

---

**ENTRY 006**
`▸ DATE: November 16, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: TNT Area housing, Point Pleasant vicinity`
`▸ TYPE: Ground Approach · Infant Present`

Marcella Bennett, accompanied by the Wamsley family, arrived at a friend's home near the TNT Area. A figure rose from behind a parked vehicle in the immediate vicinity.

Description (composite, multiple witnesses): Wings drawn close to body. Bipedal, legs consistent with human form. Head set low in the shoulders — no visible neck. Estimated height: over six feet.

During the encounter, Marcella Bennett dropped her infant daughter. The infant was unharmed. The witness's reaction is noted in the archive not as evidence of severity but as evidence of the involuntary nature of the response. Marcella Bennett did not choose to drop her child.

---

**ENTRY 007**
`▸ DATE: November 21, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Charleston, West Virginia (approx. 50 miles east of Point Pleasant)`
`▸ TYPE: Rooftop · Vertical Departure`

Witness: Richard West. Reported a six-foot figure with red eyes and a wingspan of six to eight feet observed at rest on the roof of a building. Entity departed via vertical ascent — no runway, no run-up, no glide path. Witnesses to West's account are not on record beyond West himself.

*The vertical departure is noted in this entry as a recurring anomaly. See FIELD NOTES.*

---

**ENTRY 008**
`▸ DATE: November 25, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Route 62, Mason County`
`▸ TYPE: Vehicle Pace · Self-Revised`

Witness: Tom Ury. Reported a very large bird with an estimated wingspan of ten to twelve feet that circled his vehicle and maintained pace on the road. Ury subsequently revised his account to describe the entity as a very large bird — not a supernatural entity — making him one of the few witnesses who downgraded his account over time. Both the original account and the revision are preserved in the record without editorial preference.

---

**ENTRY 009**
`▸ DATE: November 27, 1966`
`▸ LOCATION: Route 33, near New Haven, West Virginia`
`▸ TYPE: Vehicle Approach · Physical Sequela`

Witness: Connie Jo Carpenter. Entity described as seven feet tall, gray, with glowing red eyes. Wings unfolded and entity moved toward witness's vehicle. Wingspan: ten feet.

Following the encounter, Carpenter developed significant conjunctival irritation. No chemical exposure was identified as an alternative explanation. No contemporaneous medical report has been fully reconciled with a benign cause.

## ▌ ANCHOR EVENT ANALYSIS

### THE SILVER BRIDGE COLLAPSE
`▸ CASE ANCHOR DATE: December 15, 1967`
`▸ CLASSIFICATION: Verified Engineering Failure · Federal Investigation Record`

On the evening of December 15, 1967 — a Friday, five days before Christmas — the Silver Bridge spanning the Ohio River between Point Pleasant, West Virginia and Gallipolis, Ohio, failed catastrophically.

```
◈ VEHICLE COUNT AT TIME OF FAILURE:  32 vehicles
◈ PERSONS ON BRIDGE:                 64
◈ FATALITIES:                        46
◈ SURVIVORS:                         18
◈ TIME OF FAILURE:                   Approximately 5:00 PM EST
◈ INVESTIGATION DURATION:            3 years (NTSB)
◈ CAUSE DETERMINATION:               Single point failure — Eyebar #330
```

**Engineering Summary (NTSB Findings):**

The Silver Bridge employed an eyebar chain suspension system. Each link in the suspension chain consisted of two eyebars — flat steel bars with loop ends — carrying the full load of the roadway. The system contained no redundancy: failure of one eyebar in any link meant the remaining eyebar bore the full load unassisted, guaranteeing cascade failure.

Eyebar #330 developed a stress corrosion crack on the inner surface of its eye-hole — a position physically inaccessible to inspection without disassembly of the structure. The crack grew for an indeterminate period of years. The crack that initiated the collapse was, at time of failure, smaller than a human thumbnail.

**Relationship to anomaly record:**

The NTSB investigation establishes the mechanical cause of the collapse with specificity. It does not address the thirteen-month anomaly record that preceded the event. It does not account for the cessation of reported sightings on December 15, 1967 — the same date as the collapse.

These are two independently documented phenomena that share a terminal date. The Archive does not assert causality. The Archive notes coincidence.

## ▌ THEORETICAL FRAMEWORKS

Three explanatory frameworks have been advanced in the academic, journalistic, and investigative record. Each is presented here with its evidentiary basis and known limitations. Each is assigned the same verdict.

---

### FRAMEWORK-A: MISIDENTIFIED AVIAN SPECIES

**Primary advocates:** Robert L. Smith (wildlife biologist, West Virginia University, 1966); Joe Nickell (Committee for Skeptical Inquiry)

**Evidentiary basis:**
- Smith identified the Sandhill Crane as the most probable explanation: height approaching human scale, wingspan exceeding seven feet, red flesh patches around the eyes that produce measurable eyeshine under artificial lighting.
- Nickell proposed the Barred Owl as an alternative: large, nocturnal, silent in flight, strong eyeshine, known to startle observers at close range.
- Both species are real animals that produce documented alarm responses in humans who encounter them unexpectedly at night.

**Limitations:**
- Sandhill Cranes were not native to West Virginia in 1966.
- A single individual of either species cannot account for 26 sightings across four states.
- Multiple witnesses described bipedal upright stance — not consistent with crane or owl locomotion.
- No documented bird species maintains highway vehicle pace at 70–100 mph in sustained level flight without wing movement.
- No documented bird species departs vertically from a stationary position on a rooftop or parking surface.

`VERDICT: INCONCLUSIVE`
*Framework A likely accounts for a subset of reported encounters. It does not account for the full record.*

---

### FRAMEWORK-B: MASS PSYCHOGENIC ILLNESS / EXPECTANT ATTENTION

**Primary basis:** Standard model of sociogenic illness and social amplification of perceived threat

**Evidentiary basis:**
- The November 16, 1966 newspaper publication created a labeled entity in the local cultural environment. Once "Mothman" existed as a named concept, every ambiguous nighttime encounter had an available interpretive frame.
- Confirmation bias, social contagion, and the psychological pressure of shared community experience can produce sighting waves with no underlying phenomenon.
- The Point Pleasant flap follows the textbook mass hysteria timeline: trigger → media amplification → escalating reports → climactic event → cessation.
- Many of the features reported (large eyes, red reflective glow, large wingspread) can be generated by expectant attention applied to real but mundane stimuli.

**Limitations:**
- Four of the documented sightings pre-date the newspaper coverage: August 1966 (WIT-001), November 1 (WIT-002), November 12 (WIT-003, three days before the Scarberry incident), November 14 (WIT-004).
- The Chicago metropolitan flap of 2017 occurred in a population of 2.7 million with no comparable local Mothman cultural priming. The witnesses — including law enforcement and airline industry professionals — were not operating within a shared local legend framework.
- The cessation of sightings on December 15, 1967 is not adequately explained by mass hysteria models, which predict gradual decline, not abrupt termination.

`VERDICT: INCONCLUSIVE`
*Framework B likely accounts for a portion of the mid-flap expansion. It does not account for the pre-coverage sightings, the Chicago cluster, or the termination event.*

---

### FRAMEWORK-C: ULTRATERRESTRIAL / SUPERSPECTRUM ENTITY

**Primary advocate:** John A. Keel, journalist and investigator; documented in *The Mothman Prophecies* (1975) and *Strange Creatures from Time and Space* (1970)

**Evidentiary basis:**
- Keel conducted field investigation in Point Pleasant beginning late 1966. He documented anomalous phone calls, encounters with individuals possessing foreknowledge of events, and sightings consistent with a non-avian, intelligent entity.
- Keel proposed that the entities were not extraterrestrial but "ultraterrestrial" — indigenous to Earth's electromagnetic spectrum, capable of manifesting in forms appropriate to the cultural expectations of the observer. Medieval manifestations: angels and demons. Early 20th century: fairies and elementals. 1950s: flying saucers. 1966: a winged humanoid with red eyes.
- Keel's model predicts cultural modulation of appearance without alteration of underlying nature. The form changes. The presence doesn't.

**Limitations and source complications:**
- A portion of the anomalous phone calls Keel received were subsequently attributed to pranks by UFO researcher Gray Barker, who later admitted to some of this activity.
- *The Mothman Prophecies* was published eight years after the events. Keel's reconstruction incorporates hindsight bias that cannot be separated from contemporaneous observation.
- The ultraterrestrial framework is not falsifiable under current empirical methods.

**The witness problem (preserved separately from Keel):**
The witnesses in this record are not Keel. Marcella Bennett (WIT-009) is not Keel. Connie Jo Carpenter (WIT-013) is not Keel. The four occupants of the Scarberry-Mallette vehicle (WIT-005 through WIT-008) are not Keel. Their accounts existed before Keel's book and do not depend on its accuracy for their evidentiary status.

`VERDICT: INCONCLUSIVE`
*Framework C provides the most expansive explanatory scope but the least empirical grounding. The witness record stands independent of the framework.*

## ▌ FIELD NOTES

*The following is the human editorial voice of this archive. It is not objective. It is accurate.*

---

The Archive does not have a conclusion. Archives do not produce conclusions — they preserve evidence long enough for conclusions to become possible, or for the impossibility of conclusions to become clear. The Mothman case file sits in the second category for now, which is why the case status reads OPEN.

Here is what can be said without qualification:

Forty-six people died in the Ohio River on December 15, 1967. They were real. Their families were real. The grief of Point Pleasant, West Virginia was real and is documented in the photographs in Room 7, if you want to see it.

Here is what the engineering record can say without qualification: the Silver Bridge was a poorly designed structure with a single point of failure, and that failure was invisible and inaccessible for the entire operational life of the bridge. The crack that killed 46 people could not be found by any inspection method available at the time. It was growing the whole time.

Here is what the sighting record can say without qualification: from August 1966 to December 14, 1967, a substantial number of people in Mason County and the surrounding region reported encounters with something they did not have language for. They were not all the same kind of person. They were not all in the same circumstance. Some of them went to the police. Some of them never told anyone until years later. Some of them were alone. Some of them were in groups. Some of them revised their accounts downward over time. Some of them did not.

On December 15, 1967, the encounters stopped.

The Archive does not assert that the creature was a warning. The Archive does not assert that the creature caused the collapse. The Archive does not assert that the creature was a creature.

The Archive asserts that the temporal coincidence of the flap's termination and the disaster's occurrence is documented, and that no explanatory framework in this record accounts for it satisfactorily.

---

**On the recurring vertical departure:**

Five witnesses in this file — WIT-011 (Charleston, 1966), CHI-003 (Chicago, 2017), CHI-004 (O'Hare, 2019), CHI-005 (O'Hare, 2020), and an uncounted number of secondary accounts — describe a departure that is entirely vertical. Straight up. No run, no wind assist, no glide path. Observers consistently struggle to describe it. "Like a helicopter" is the most common comparison. Some use the word "launched."

No bird does this. The physics of avian flight require horizontal velocity for lift generation. There is no ornithological explanation for a large bird departing vertically from a stationary position on the ground or a structural surface.

This detail appears in accounts separated by fifty-four years and fifteen hundred miles. It is the one consistent physical anomaly that no framework resolves.

---

**On the witnesses:**

This file uses real names where they are in the public record. It does not assess the reliability of those individuals beyond the evidentiary notes provided. It does not dismiss any account for failing to fit a preferred framework.

The man who wrote "I don't imagine anyone would believe me anyways" before going back to work on a night shift at the Chicago International Produce Market — that man's account is in this file. It will stay in this file. He was not wrong about being disbelieved, and the Archive notes that fact.

---

**Final position:**

The case is open.

The log is open.

Something happened. The record shows that much. What the record does not show — what it may never show — is what that something was.

The Museum of Ongoing Events does not close its files on the basis of discomfort with ambiguity. It closes files when the question is answered.

This one is not answered.
