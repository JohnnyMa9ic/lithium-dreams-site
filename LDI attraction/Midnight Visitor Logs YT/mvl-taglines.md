# Midnight Visitor Logs — Taglines, Catchphrases & Brand Voice
### Lithium Dreams TV | Standalone Concept Document v1.0

---

## Purpose

This document captures the repeatable brand language for *Midnight Visitor Logs*. It is divided into three layers:

1. **Series-Level Taglines** — Identity phrases that define the show, used consistently across thumbnails, intros, social posts, and outros.
2. **Episode-Specific Hooks** — Subject-tailored openers and hooks for individual episodes. The Mothman episode is the anchor example; the framework scales to any topic.
3. **Catchphrase Architecture** — Guidelines for generating new catchphrases for any future episode using the same voice and logic.

---

## The Show's Voice — In One Sentence

> *A man from the middle of the country who loves facts, loves mysteries, and refuses to let either one kill the other.*

Every phrase in this document lives at that intersection.

## Layer 1: Series-Level Taglines

These are evergreen. Use them on channel art, in the outro, as pinned comments, and in social media bios.

---

### Primary Tagline

> **"The log is open. The night is long. Let's go in."**

*Use for:* Channel trailer, series intro card, episode cold-open callback. Sets the tone — investigative, nocturnal, welcoming, slightly daring.

---

### Secondary Taglines (choose by context)

> **"Facts don't care what you believe. But they can't explain everything either."**

*Use for:* YouTube channel description, "About" section, press kit. This is the philosophical north star of the show in one sentence.

---

> **"We don't tell you what to think. We just turn the flashlight on."**

*Use for:* Thumbnail text overlay, end card. Establishes trust with skeptical viewers without alienating believers.

---

> **"Every legend started as somebody's Tuesday night."**

*Use for:* Social media posts, episode teasers. Grounds the supernatural in the mundane — which is where the best horror lives.

---

> **"You don't have to believe it. But you can't un-hear it."**

*Use for:* End of cold opens, pre-roll ad copy, episode descriptions. Creates a hook that works regardless of where the viewer lands on the belief spectrum.

---

> **"Come curious. Leave unsettled."**

*Use for:* Merch, social bio, show tagline card. Short enough to fit on a shirt. Honest enough to be a real promise.

---

### Series Signoff (Outro — use every episode, verbatim)

> **"Keep the log open. I'll see you in the dark."**

*This is the equivalent of "That's all, folks" for this show — consistent, unmistakable, and slightly eerie without being corny. After 10 episodes it becomes a ritual for your audience.*

## Layer 3: Catchphrase Architecture — Scale to Any Episode

When generating taglines and catchphrases for a new episode, run through this checklist. Every good *Midnight Visitor Logs* phrase hits at least 3 of these 5 triggers:

### The Five Triggers

| # | Trigger | What it does | Example |
|---|---------|--------------|---------|
| 1 | **The Tension Pivot** | Juxtaposes a fact with its unexplainable implication | *"The crack was smaller than a thumbnail. The bridge was 1,750 feet long."* |
| 2 | **The Witness Transfer** | Puts the viewer in the witness's position | *"You're driving home. It's 11:30. And something keeps up with your car."* |
| 3 | **The Permission Line** | Explicitly tells the audience they don't have to believe — which paradoxically makes them lean in | *"I'm not asking you to accept this. I'm asking you to sit with it."* |
| 4 | **The Plain-Spoken Drop** | A simple, flat Midwestern statement that carries more weight than its word count suggests | *"Forty-six people. December 15th. Rush hour."* |
| 5 | **The Open Door** | Ends on a question or invitation, never a verdict | *"I don't know what they saw. But I know they all used the same words."* |

---

### Catchphrase Generation Template

For any new episode subject, fill in the blanks:

```
TENSION PIVOT:
"[Mundane factual detail]. [Number that makes it real]. [Implication no one can explain]."

WITNESS TRANSFER:
"You're [everyday activity]. [Time or place]. And [the impossible thing happens]."

PERMISSION LINE:
"I'm not here to tell you [X]. I'm here to show you [Y] and let you [decide / sit with it / take it home]."

PLAIN-SPOKEN DROP:
"[Number]. [Date]. [Blunt statement of what happened]."

OPEN DOOR:
"I don't know what [they / it / this] [was / means / is]. 
But [specific detail that defies easy explanation]."
```

## Usage Guidelines

**Consistency is everything.** The series signoff — *"Keep the log open. I'll see you in the dark."* — must appear verbatim at the close of every episode. This is how audiences form rituals with shows. It's the reason people quote their favorite hosts.

**Don't over-tagline.** One series tagline in the intro. One episode hook in the cold open. Two to three callback lines within the episode. One signoff. That's the full load. More than that dilutes the impact.

**The host never winks.** The Midwest voice works because it's earnest. The moment the host sounds like he's performing mystery instead of feeling it, the whole thing deflates. Every phrase should read like something the host actually thinks, said plainly.
