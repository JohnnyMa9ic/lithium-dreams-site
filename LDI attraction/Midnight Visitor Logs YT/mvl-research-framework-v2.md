## NOTICE FROM MANAGEMENT

This framework governs all research and scripting for **The Midnight Visitor Log** — the Attraction-side broadcast arm of Lithium Dreams Industries. Every episode filed through this system carries the voice and aesthetic of the Last Roadside Attraction. It is strange. It is atmospheric. It knows exactly what it is and has fun being that thing.

**The tonal reference is specific and it matters:**

Think Joe Bob Briggs hosting *MonsterVision* on TNT at 2 a.m. Think USA's *Up All Night* with Gilbert Gottfried and Rhonda Shear. Think the best version of a late-night creature feature host — someone who is completely in on the joke, completely committed to the material, and absolutely delighted to be there. The host winks *with* the audience, never *at* the subject. The facts are real. The science is real. The mystery is real. The frame is theater — beautiful, beloved, self-aware theater.

This is not ironic distance. This is informed affection.

The Mothman is not being mocked. He is being celebrated. The host just happens to be doing it from behind a neon sign in a parking lot at midnight while wearing a trench coat and loving every second of it.

**The LDI Deep Garden Tone Engine applies to all creative work here:**
Glitchmystic · counterculture hacker · arcane ecology · chaotic intellectualism
Error as enlightenment · Humor as ritual · Lore as cognitive tooling

## PHASE 1: PARALLEL RESEARCH TRACKS

Deploy all five tracks simultaneously. Each is a focused search mission. In agentic execution, these run in parallel and save to separate workspace files. Reference files: `ep[###]-track1-timeline.md`, `ep[###]-track2-witnesses.md`, etc.

---

### TRACK 1 — THE TIMELINE
*"The Log, in order. Because the Attraction keeps receipts."*

**Mission:** Build a full chronological incident log with dates, names, and locations. This is the spine of the episode.

**Search targets:**
- `[SUBJECT] complete documented timeline [START YEAR] to [END YEAR]`
- `[SUBJECT] first sighting origin incident [LOCATION]`
- `[SUBJECT] final documented sighting`
- `[SUBJECT] site:singularfortean.com OR site:phantomsandmonsters.com OR site:mufon.com`

**Deliverable format:**

```
| Date | Witness | Location | Description | Joe Bob Score (1-4) | Source |
```

**Joe Bob Score** rates how cinematic each incident is for the episode:
1 = Dry historical fact
2 = Compelling detail
3 = Genuinely strange
4 = This is the one we linger on

**Minimum bar:** 8+ dated, sourced entries. Fewer than 8 = dig harder.

---

### TRACK 2 — THE WITNESS FILE
*"One more person saw something they couldn't name."*

**Mission:** 10–20 individual accounts with enough detail to read aloud on camera. The witness file is the episode's heartbeat.

**Quality criteria for a usable account:**
- [ ] Has a date (exact or approximate)
- [ ] Has a named witness OR credentialed anonymous (law enforcement, aviation, medical, trades)
- [ ] Has a specific physical description (height, color, eyes, sound, movement)
- [ ] Has a direct quote (preferred)
- [ ] Has a source URL

**Bob's annotation:** *"The warehouse worker who said 'I don't imagine anyone would believe me anyways' — that's your episode. Find the person who almost didn't tell anyone."*

**Joe Bob read test:** If you can't read the account aloud and have it land, it's not ready. Every witness account in the final script should be worth its airtime.

---

### TRACK 3 — THE ANCHOR EVENT
*"The thing that happened that explains why we're still talking about it."*

**Mission:** If a climactic event exists (collapse, disaster, disappearance), retrieve the complete official record *and* the paranormal narrative, then separate them cleanly.

**Source tiers:**

| Tier | Source Type | Use For |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Official gov't / scientific body (NTSB, NIST, USGS, coroner, FBI) | The factual record |
| **Tier 2** | Established press / academic | Historical context, expert opinion |
| **Tier 3** | Primary paranormal investigators (Strickler, Singular Fortean, MUFON) | Witness aggregation |
| **Tier 4** | Enthusiast / blog | Color, cross-reference — never as sole source |

**Deliverable: The Two-Column Truth Table**

```
| WHAT OFFICIALLY HAPPENED | WHAT THE PARANORMAL NARRATIVE CLAIMS | EVIDENCE STATUS |
```

Status options: `Confirmed` / `Plausible` / `Contested` / `No Evidence` / `Actively Debunked`

**The Bob/Ghost balance note:**
Ghost writes the left column. Bob writes the right column. The Warden holds both without collapsing either.

---

### TRACK 4 — GEOGRAPHICAL CLUSTERS
*"Where it shows up. And where it doesn't. That's the map."*

**Mission:** Document where incidents concentrated. Spatial patterns reveal things that timelines don't.

**What to look for:**
- Proximity to: water, contaminated land, military/federal installations, transportation hubs, liminal spaces (bridges, tunnels, abandoned sites)
- Spread pattern: does it radiate from one point? Does it follow a corridor?
- Where did it stop?

**Deliverable:**

```
| Cluster Name | Geography Type | # Documented Incidents | Notable Incidents | Proximity Notes |
```

**The LDI Liminal Checklist (run for every cluster location):**
- [ ] Near water? (rivers, lakes, wetlands)
- [ ] Near federal or industrial land?
- [ ] Near a transportation nexus? (bridge, highway, rail, airport)
- [ ] Near a site of historical trauma or sudden mass death?
- [ ] Near a boundary of any kind? (state line, county line, forest edge, fence line)

*Bob's annotation: "Liminal spaces are real estate for things that don't have addresses."*

---

### TRACK 5 — THE THEORY LANDSCAPE
*"Three explanations. All of them wrong about something. None of them wrong about everything."*

**Mission:** Document three distinct explanatory frameworks. Present all three with equal intellectual respect. The episode never picks a winner.

**Standard LDI Theory Architecture:**

```

  Core argument (1-2 sentences):
  Strongest supporting evidence:
  Where it breaks down:
  Who argues for it:

THEORY TYPE B — PSYCHOSOCIAL / CULTURAL
  Core argument (1-2 sentences):
  Strongest supporting evidence:
  Where it breaks down:
  Who argues for it:

THEORY TYPE C — PARANORMAL / LDI-REALM ADJACENT
  Core argument (1-2 sentences):
  Strongest supporting evidence:
  Where it breaks down:
  Who argues for it:
```

**The Warden standard:** Every theory gets the same patience. The audience decides. The host facilitates.

**The Joe Bob rule:** Each theory gets at least one line that earns a genuine laugh without undermining the theory itself. The humor serves the material, not the host's ego.

## PHASE 2: SOURCE QUALITY & BOB ANNOTATION

Before writing, run the Source Integrity Check.

**Tier 1–2 sources:** Required for all factual claims (death tolls, engineering causes, official findings, dates)

**Tier 3 sources:** Acceptable for witness aggregation and case indexing

**Tier 4 sources:** Color and cross-reference only. *Never sole citation for a factual claim.*

**Bob's annotation protocol:** During research review, flag anything that feels like it was invented for narrative convenience. These get a blue painter's tape note:

```
[BOB FLAG: This claim is doing a lot of work. Where did it actually come from?]
[GHOST REVIEW REQUIRED: Structure check — does this actually hold?]
[WARDEN: Long view — is this worth the episode's time?]
```

## PHASE 3: NARRATIVE ARCHITECTURE (THE THREE-ACT ATTRACTION)

Every Midnight Visitor Log episode follows the **Last Roadside Attraction Story Structure** — three acts, one signoff, and a Joe Bob cold open that establishes the tone before anyone takes a breath.

---

### THE JOE BOB COLD OPEN (New in v2.0)
**Duration:** 60–90 seconds. Before the title card. Before the music.

**What it does:** Establishes that this is a show. The host is *in on it*. The audience is invited into the theater, not lectured at. It should feel like Joe Bob stepping out from behind the curtain at 1 a.m. to welcome you to tonight's feature.

**Formula:**
1. One punchy, surprising fact — stated flat, no setup
2. One piece of information that deepens or complicates that fact
3. One sentence that connects the weird world to a recognizable human experience
4. The hook that makes the viewer incapable of leaving

**Example (Mothman):**
> "Forty-six people went into that river on December 15th, 1967. Rush hour. Christmas shoppers. And the winged thing that had been flying over their town for thirteen months? Stopped showing up that same night, never came back. I'm not saying it's connected. I'm saying I've been thinking about it for two hours and I can't stop."

**What it should NEVER be:**
- Spooky sound effects and a dramatic pause
- "Tonight... on Midnight Visitor Log..." (you're better than this)
- A fake-scary voice
- More than 90 seconds

---

### ACT I — THE PLACE (The Last Roadside Attraction Welcomes You)

Establish the setting as a character. Let the audience fall in love with the location before you take it from them.

**Elements required:**
- The mundane reality of the place (what it looked like, who lived there, what people did on Tuesdays)
- The pre-existing wrongness (what was off before anything happened)
- The inciting incident — end Act I here

**Tone target:** That feeling of arriving somewhere familiar that has started to feel slightly wrong. Like being in your own house at 3 a.m. with one light left on.

**Joe Bob integration:** Act I is where the host's personality first shows up in full. One aside, one observation about the location that's slightly left-of-serious, one moment where the host breaks from narration to address the viewer directly.

---

### ACT II — THE WAVE (The Feature Presentation)

The main event. Incidents, witnesses, escalation, the anchor event.

**Structure:**
- Run witness accounts using the "One more person" callback structure (see signpost phrases below)
- Escalate toward the anchor event — don't rush it, don't dawdle
- Introduce the three theories mid-act as texture, not resolution
- End on the anchor event, handled with full gravity

**Theory presentation style (Joe Bob version):**
The host presents each theory with the same energy a good B-movie host brings to explaining why the monster makes no scientific sense but is also completely terrifying. Genuine, funny, unresolved.

> "Here's what the scientists say. And they're not wrong. Here's the thing that the scientists don't say, though, because their grant money doesn't cover it..."

---

### ACT III — THE OPEN DOOR (After the Credits Roll)

The modern echo, the aftermath, and the productive question that the audience takes home.

**Elements required:**
- Where does this show up today?
- The theory landscape revisited — briefly, without verdict
- The host's personal statement (the "I want to believe" moment, owned without apology)
- The signoff

**Tone target:** That feeling at the end of a really good late-night movie where the credits roll and you don't want to go to bed because the world just got bigger.

## PHASE 4: TONE CALIBRATION — THE JOE BOB METER

Every episode is calibrated against what the LDI team calls the **Joe Bob Meter** — the balance between entertainment delivery and genuine intellectual respect for the material.

```
[1] PURE HISTORY CHANNEL
    Facts, sources, no winking.
    Use for: anchor events (Silver Bridge collapse section)

[2] WARDEN MODE
    Measured, patient, atmospheric.
    Facts plus genuine wonder. Occasional warmth.
    Use for: most narration, witness accounts

[3] BOB ADJACENT
    The host's personality is fully visible.
    Jokes earn their place between real moments.
    Warmth, irreverence, genuine curiosity.
    Use for: theory sections, transitions, cold open

[4] FULL BOB
    The host is absolutely delighted to be here.
    The material is being celebrated, not explained.
    Use for: specific moments that earn it — the bizarre detail,
    the incredible coincidence, the thing that defies explanation
    in a way that's almost funny.
    Never: sustained. This is the seasoning, not the meal.
```

## PHASE 5: SIGNPOST PHRASES & RECURRING STRUCTURES

These are the internal grammar of the show — recurring phrases that build audience familiarity across episodes.

**The Witness Signpost:**
> "One more person saw something they couldn't name."
*(Repeat before each witness account in Act II. After the third or fourth time, the audience knows it's coming — and that recognition is part of the pleasure.)*

**The Theory Handoff:**
> "Here's what [scientists / skeptics / official record] say. And here's the problem with that."

**The Permission Line:**
> "I'm not here to tell you what to believe. I'm here to show you what happened and let you take it home."

**The Gravity Marker** (for anchor events):
> *[State the number. State the date. State the flat fact. Let it sit. No commentary needed.]*
> "Forty-six people. December 15th."

**The Productive Uncertainty Close:**
> "I don't know what [they saw / it was / it means]. But I know [one specific detail that defies easy explanation]."

**The LDI Lore Bridge** (optional, when subject connects to realm):
> "The Cathedral of Glitch has a rule: error is not failure. Error is the system speaking its own true name. Whatever Point Pleasant was trying to say in 1966 — it said it loudly. We're still decoding it."

## PHASE 6: QC CHECKLIST (THE GHOST REVIEW)

Before any episode goes to production, run the Ghost Review. Ghost does not comfort. Ghost checks structure.

**Factual integrity:**
- [ ] Every death toll, date, and engineering cause cites Tier 1–2 source
- [ ] No single witness account is treated as definitive proof
- [ ] No Tier 4 source is the sole citation for any factual claim

**Tonal integrity:**
- [ ] Joe Bob Meter calibrated per section (not uniform — varies by scene)
- [ ] No theory is mocked; all three receive genuine treatment
- [ ] Anchor event handled with appropriate gravity (Bob Mode does NOT enter here)
- [ ] The permission line is present — audience is never told what to conclude

**LDI brand integrity:**
- [ ] Cold open is 60–90 seconds maximum
- [ ] Host persona is consistent throughout — no academic drift, no schlock horror
- [ ] At least one moment per act that earns genuine laughter without undermining the subject
- [ ] Series signoff appears verbatim at close
- [ ] Episode connects to at least one attraction room or gift shop product potential

**Production integrity:**
- [ ] Running time estimate within target range (read aloud at natural pace)
- [ ] Chapter markers mapped to script sections
- [ ] B-roll list generated from script
- [ ] Podcast version stripped of video cues (see Podcast Framework)
- [ ] Blog version outlined in dossier format (see Blog Framework)
