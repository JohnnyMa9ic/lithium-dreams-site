*All blog posts published under the Midnight Visitor Log brand are formatted as dossier files — official-looking, cold-structured, institutional in layout. The content is strange. The container is precise. This is the Weyland-Yutani aesthetic applied to roadside folklore: if the Company had a department for cryptids, this is how their memos would look.*

*The X-Files Mulder's folder reference applies to everything below the classification header. Once you're inside the case file, the writing warms — facts first, wonder second, host voice third. The container is cold. The content is alive.*

## THE DOSSIER FORMAT

Every blog post follows this exact structural template. The headers are design elements as much as they are organizational ones. Written as if filed by an institution that takes anomalous reports seriously and processes them through a formal intake system.

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY BLOCK

```
▌ EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
```

Two to three paragraphs. Cold, precise opening paragraph in institutional voice — what happened, where, when, how many incidents, current status. Second paragraph shifts into the host's genuine voice — what makes this matter, what the human weight of it is. Third paragraph (optional): the productive question this dossier will leave open.

This is where the Weyland-Yutani container first cracks and the human being inside it becomes visible.

## TYPOGRAPHY NOTES

The dossier format relies on specific typographic choices to maintain the Weyland-Yutani / X-Files institutional aesthetic while remaining readable on the web.

**In Markdown / Ghost CMS:**
- Use `━━━` (box drawing characters) for horizontal rules — not standard `---`
- Use `▌` prefix for all major section headers
- Use `┌─┐ │ └─┘` box drawing for witness cards
- Use `>` blockquote for all direct witness quotes
- Use `[CAPS]` for status indicators and classifications

**In Ghost CMS / HTML rendering:**
- Target font: IBM Plex Mono or JetBrains Mono for header blocks, code blocks, and classification lines
- Body font: IBM Plex Sans or Inter
- Color scheme: Dark background with amber/green terminal accent — mirrors the LDI brand palette
- Headers in Bank Gothic or Eurostile if available

## CONVERSION FROM VIDEO SCRIPT

The blog/article version is derived from the video script in four passes:

**Pass 1 — Strip:**
Remove all stage directions, B-roll cues, and camera directions

**Pass 2 — Header-ize:**
Wrap the institutional header block around the content; convert section breaks to `▌ SECTION` headers

**Pass 3 — Witness Cards:**
Convert witness account narration into structured incident cards with classification status

**Pass 4 — Investigator's Note:**
The video outro becomes the blog's closing Investigator's Note — same words, same voice, formatted as a direct-address break from the institutional container
