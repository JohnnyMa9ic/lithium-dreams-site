## 1. SERVICE TIERS

Glasshouse operates on four engagement levels. Each tier is a complete thing — not a stripped-down version of a higher tier. The right tier depends on where a client is standing, not on what they can afford to aspire to.

---

### TIER I — The Orientation

**Engagement Model:** Fixed-fee consultation  
**Price Range:** $500 – $1,200 (single session or two-session package)  
**Typical Timeline:** 1–2 weeks from booking to deliverable

**Who It's For**
Operators who know something isn't working but can't yet name it. New to AI tools, or have tried a few things that didn't stick. Team of 1–5. The Intake Wizard route: "Not Sure Yet" or first-time explorers of any track.

**What's Included**
- 90-minute recorded working session (Situation Map)
- Pre-session questionnaire + context review
- Written System Brief: current state, friction points, opportunity inventory
- Recommended track (which Glasshouse system applies to their situation)
- A prioritized "three moves" action plan — specific, not hypothetical
- 30-day email access for follow-up questions

**What's NOT Included**
- Any build work
- Notion workspace setup
- Automation configuration
- Ongoing access or retainer

**Example Engagement**
A licensed therapist running a private practice in Portland. She's been using Google Docs for session notes, a paper calendar for scheduling, and a Gmail inbox for client intake — and she's drowning. She doesn't know what "automation" means in practice but suspects there's a better way. The Orientation gives her clarity: which one problem to solve first, what tool actually fits her, and a specific recommendation (Local Business Ops Pilot) before she spends a dollar more.

---

### TIER II — The Pilot Build

**Engagement Model:** Fixed-scope project  
**Price Range:** $3,500 – $8,500 (depending on system complexity)  
**Typical Timeline:** 3–5 weeks

**Who It's For**
Operators who know what they need help with and are ready to make one focused investment. Moderate AI comfort — they've used ChatGPT or similar, but haven't built anything. Team of 1–15. This is the most common entry point for clients who've already done an Orientation or arrived via referral.

**What's Included**
- One fully configured system (Research OS, Content Engine, Local Business Ops, or Executive Assistant OS)
- Notion workspace setup with the relevant template suite (3–5 templates)
- One automation harness (intake, research, content, or briefing — matched to the system)
- Brand Voice Reference or Source Registry (whichever applies)
- Documentation: written SOP for how the system works
- Two training sessions (recorded)
- 30-day post-launch support window

**What's NOT Included**
- Second system builds
- Ongoing content creation or research work
- Infrastructure maintenance (API keys, server upkeep) after the 30-day window
- Additional harness configurations

**Example Engagement**
A boutique commercial real estate broker in Nashville, 3-person team. They generate leads from cold outreach, referrals, and their newsletter — but have no system for tracking any of it. They need Local Business Ops: lead intake tracking, a follow-up sequence they can actually stick to, and a client onboarding checklist. Five weeks later, they have a working Notion CRM, an automated intake form that routes inquiries to the right team member, and a follow-up sequence that sends without anyone thinking about it.

---

### TIER III — The Operating System

**Engagement Model:** Project build + ongoing retainer  
**Price Range:** $12,000 – $28,000 build / $1,200 – $3,500/month retainer  
**Typical Timeline:** 8–14 weeks for full build; retainer begins at launch

**Who It's For**
Operators running a real business with multiple moving parts who are ready to systematize across the board. They've likely already seen what a single system can do (through their own experimentation or a Pilot Build) and want the whole picture. Team of 5–40. AI comfort: moderate to advancing. They are not becoming technologists — but they're done doing things manually.

**What's Included**
- Full multi-system build (2–4 systems from the catalog, configured together)
- Notion master workspace with all relevant template suites
- Up to 3 harness configurations (e.g., Research + Content + Operator Briefing)
- Cross-system integration (data flows between systems — the research brief feeds the content calendar)
- Hermes orchestration layer (where applicable)
- Full documentation suite: system map, SOPs, training library
- Monthly architecture review session (1 hour)
- Priority support channel (Slack or email)
- Quarterly system audit + optimization

**What's NOT Included**
- Building custom software or apps outside the Glasshouse stack
- Managing third-party vendor relationships (social platforms, email providers)
- Paid API cost coverage (client owns their own API accounts)

**Example Engagement**
A 12-person wealth management firm in Chicago. The principals want AI-assisted research briefs on their client industries, a content engine for their LinkedIn newsletter, and a weekly briefing system for the managing director. They also need their client intake process modernized. Over 12 weeks, Glasshouse builds Research OS, Content Engine, and Local Business Ops in a unified Notion workspace, with harnesses running in the background. The MD gets a 7am briefing every Monday. The newsletter goes from "we keep meaning to do it" to a published cadence. The intake form has never touched a paper form since.

---

### TIER IV — The Embedded Studio

**Engagement Model:** Deep retainer / fractional systems partnership  
**Price Range:** $5,000 – $12,000/month  
**Typical Timeline:** Ongoing (minimum 6-month commitment)

**Who It's For**
Established businesses (or fast-growing ones) that want Glasshouse as an embedded systems partner — not a vendor. The operator doesn't want to manage a technology stack; they want to think, decide, and have the system handle the infrastructure. Team of 10–100+. AI comfort: varies — this tier exists precisely because the operator doesn't need to care.

**What's Included**
- Everything in The Operating System, continuously
- Hermes agentic layer fully managed and maintained by Glasshouse
- Proactive system expansion as the business grows or pivots
- New harness configurations as needs emerge (no extra build fees within retainer scope)
- Monthly architecture session + ad hoc access during business hours
- System health monitoring (Glasshouse watches for failures, API drift, automation breaks)
- Annual full system review and rebuild proposal
- Direct line to John (signal routing and orchestration decisions happen at this tier)

**What's NOT Included**
- Staff augmentation (Glasshouse builds systems, not headcount)
- Legal, accounting, or domain-specific expert advisory
- Content creation (the system drafts; the client publishes)

**Example Engagement**
A regional property management company, 45 employees across 3 markets. They manage 800+ units and have two administrative staff who are drowning. Glasshouse embeds as the invisible systems layer: automated maintenance request routing, tenant communications on a cadence, owner reporting on a schedule, and a competitive market watch that the asset manager reads every Tuesday morning. No one at the company calls it "AI" — they just call it Tuesday.

## 2. PRODUCT CATALOG — PREBUILT SYSTEMS

These are production-ready system configurations. Each one has been run internally at LDI and is offered as a starting point, not a template. Every deployment is configured to the client's actual situation.

### KNOWLEDGE SYSTEMS (Research OS)

#### The Signal Stack
**Tagline:** Your information environment, finally under control.

**Problem It Solves:** You read constantly but retain little. Your research is scattered across browser tabs, email newsletters, Slack links, and half-remembered podcasts. When you need to know what's happening in your space, you start from zero.

**What's Included**
- Source Registry (categorized list of publications, feeds, experts, competitive signals)
- Daily Signal Brief template (auto-populated digest, reviewed in under 10 minutes)
- Synthesis Workspace (structured space for turning reads into decisions)
- Automated daily brief pipeline (Perplexity API + Claude + Notion)
- Topic alert configuration (trigger on specific keywords or sources)

**Who It's For:** Independent consultants, analysts, executives, investors, and any operator who needs to stay current in their field without drowning in information.

**Price:** $4,200 (project) / $450/month (harness maintenance, optional)  
**Time to Deploy:** 2–3 weeks  
**In the Wild:** LDI internal research operations — John's daily brief has run on this stack since 2024. Every research engagement is scoped from a Signal Stack brief.

---

#### The Competitive Watch Room
**Tagline:** Know what your competitors are doing before your clients ask.

**Problem It Solves:** You track competitors inconsistently — a Google Alert here, a LinkedIn check there. You have no structured way to spot patterns, and when something changes in your market, you often hear about it late.

**What's Included**
- Competitive Watch Dashboard (5–10 tracked entities)
- Weekly summary brief (what changed, what to watch)
- Alert configuration for significant moves (funding, hiring, pricing, product launches)
- Synthesis template for strategic implications
- Quarterly competitive landscape report format

**Who It's For:** Business development leads, founders, strategy consultants, marketing directors at companies where knowing the competitive landscape isn't optional.

**Price:** $3,800 (project) / $380/month (harness maintenance, optional)  
**Time to Deploy:** 2 weeks  
**In the Wild:** Used across LDI's advisory engagements to track studio and agency competitive positioning.

---

#### The Institutional Memory
**Tagline:** Stop losing what your team already knows.

**Problem It Solves:** Knowledge lives in people's heads, email threads, and folders that nobody can find. When someone leaves — or just goes on vacation — the organization loses access to years of context.

**What's Included**
- Master Knowledge Base structure (Notion)
- Document intake workflow (new information lands in the right place automatically)
- Tagging and retrieval system
- "Who knows what" directory integration
- Onboarding reading path generator (for new team members)

**Who It's For:** Teams of 5–50 where institutional knowledge loss is a real operational risk.

**Price:** $5,500 (project)  
**Time to Deploy:** 3–4 weeks  
**In the Wild:** LDI's own operating documentation — everything from vendor relationships to past project briefs lives in a version of this system.

### AUTOMATION SYSTEMS (Local Business Ops)

#### The Front Door
**Tagline:** Your intake process, automated from the first inquiry.

**Problem It Solves:** New leads come in through email, contact forms, referrals, and DMs — and fall through the cracks because there's no consistent process for what happens next. Follow-up is inconsistent. Qualification is informal. Good leads get lost.

**What's Included**
- Intake form (Tally or Typeform, configured to the business)
- Cloudflare Worker pipeline (form → JSON → Notion → email sequence trigger)
- Lead Intake Tracker (Notion CRM)
- Automated qualification routing (AI scores and routes based on configured criteria)
- Initial response email template (personalized by Claude, reviewed before send)
- Follow-up Sequence Manager (3–5 touch cadence)

**Who It's For:** Service businesses with inbound inquiries: consultants, agencies, contractors, therapists, coaches, salons, studios.

**Price:** $4,500 (project) / $350/month (harness maintenance, optional)  
**Time to Deploy:** 2–3 weeks  
**In the Wild:** The Glasshouse Mission Intake Wizard itself — the six-step intake form that routes client inquiries into John's review queue is built on this exact pipeline.

---

#### The Delivery Rail
**Tagline:** From signed to delivered, without the coordination overhead.

**Problem It Solves:** Once a client signs, the internal process gets fuzzy. Who sends the onboarding email? When does the kickoff happen? What does the client need before work can start? Projects lose momentum in the first two weeks because nobody tracked it.

**What's Included**
- Client Onboarding Checklist (Notion — triggers on new client creation)
- Service Delivery Tracker (project phases, milestones, owner assignments)
- Automated onboarding email sequence (3-part welcome series)
- Client-facing status update template (weekly touchpoint)
- Offboarding + handoff checklist

**Who It's For:** Service businesses that deliver projects or recurring services: agencies, consultants, contractors, studios.

**Price:** $3,800 (project)  
**Time to Deploy:** 2 weeks  
**In the Wild:** Glasshouse's own project delivery system — every Pilot Build and OS engagement runs on this rail.

**Tagline:** Your operations calendar, running without reminders.

**Problem It Solves:** Recurring operational tasks — invoicing, supply orders, compliance check-ins, staff reviews, maintenance schedules — exist in someone's memory instead of the system. Things get missed not because nobody cares, but because nobody was watching.

### OPERATOR DASHBOARDS (Executive Assistant OS)

#### The Weekly Brief System
**Tagline:** Monday morning clarity, without anyone having to prepare it.

**Problem It Solves:** The executive starts every week reactive — clearing email, checking what's on the calendar, trying to remember what was decided last week. There's no compiled view of what matters this week. The first hour is spent assembling context that should already be there.

**What's Included**
- Weekly Mission Brief template (auto-populated Sunday night)
- Calendar pull + priority interpretation (what are the real commitments this week?)
- Email surface scan (what needs a decision? what's waiting on you?)
- Prior week close-out summary
- Horizon Planner (30/60/90 view, updated automatically)
- Operator Briefing Harness (Gmail API + Google Calendar API + Claude + Notion)

**Who It's For:** Executives, principals, and senior operators who have too much to track and not enough time to assemble context every week.

**Price:** $5,200 (project) / $520/month (harness maintenance)  
**Time to Deploy:** 2–3 weeks  
**In the Wild:** The LDI principal's own operating system — Bob (the internal Quarterback agent) compiles a version of this every Monday morning.

#### The Decision Architecture
**Tagline:** Build an institutional memory for your best thinking.

**Problem It Solves:** Decisions get made but not recorded. Six months later, nobody remembers why the pricing changed, who was in the room, or what the alternative was. Revisiting decisions happens because context was lost, not because the decision was wrong.

**What's Included**
- Decision Log (Notion database — what was decided, why, what was considered, what to revisit)
- Meeting brief template (before the meeting: context, stakes, decision needed)
- Post-meeting capture template (what was decided, next steps, open questions)
- Stakeholder Map (who needs to know what, and how)
- AI-assisted meeting notes processing (raw transcript → structured log entry)

**Who It's For:** Executives, board members, founding teams, and consultants who make high-stakes decisions regularly and need to be able to reconstruct their reasoning.

**Price:** $3,500 (project)  
**Time to Deploy:** 1–2 weeks  
**In the Wild:** LDI's internal project architecture — every major product decision has a corresponding log entry that traces the reasoning.

## 3. CONSULTATION & IDEATION SERVICES

These services deliver thinking, architecture, and clarity — not built systems. They are sometimes the right move before a build, and sometimes the right move instead of one.

---

### The Situation Map
**What It Is:** A structured discovery and audit session. Glasshouse interviews the operator, reviews their current tools and workflows, and produces a written map of what's working, what isn't, and where the leverage points are.

**What You Get:** 90-minute session (recorded) + written Situation Map document (4–6 pages): current state inventory, friction taxonomy, opportunity ranking, recommended next move.

**Who It's For:** Any operator before a significant systems investment. Also useful for operators who've already built something and aren't sure why it isn't sticking.

**Price:** $750 standalone / included in Tier I (The Orientation)  
**Timeline:** Deliverable within 5 business days of session

---

### The System Design
**What It Is:** Architecture without build. Glasshouse designs a complete system — what it would contain, how it would connect, what harnesses would run it — delivered as a specification document the client can use immediately or have Glasshouse (or another builder) implement.

**What You Get:** Full system specification: Notion workspace architecture, template inventory, automation flow diagram, harness configuration spec, implementation checklist.

**Who It's For:** Operators who have an internal builder or technical resource and want Glasshouse's architecture thinking without the full build engagement. Also useful for Tier III/IV clients before a scope is finalized.

**Price:** $2,200 – $4,500 (depending on system scope)  
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

---

### The AI Readiness Assessment
**What It Is:** An honest evaluation of whether a business is ready to get value from AI automation — and if not, what they need to do first. Most businesses that struggle with AI tools aren't struggling because the tools are bad. They're struggling because their underlying operations aren't systematized enough to automate.

**What You Get:** Written AI Readiness Report: data infrastructure score, workflow maturity score, team capability assessment, a clear "ready / not yet ready" verdict for each proposed use case, and a prioritized preparation plan.

**Who It's For:** Operators who are being pitched AI tools by vendors and want an independent view. Also for leadership teams about to make a significant investment in automation and want to know if the foundation is in place.

**Price:** $1,200  
**Timeline:** One week (includes pre-session questionnaire + 60-minute review call)

---

### Advisory Retainer
**What It Is:** Ongoing access to Glasshouse's operator thinking. Not a help desk. Not a tech support line. A standing relationship with a systems architect who knows your operation — for when you need a second mind on a decision, a gut-check on a vendor pitch, or a thinking partner on a systems challenge.

**What You Get:** 2 hours/month of advisory access (async or synchronous), priority response on questions, access to all new Glasshouse templates and documentation as produced, annual strategic review session.

**Who It's For:** Operators who've graduated from a Pilot Build or OS engagement and want to maintain a connection without committing to a full embedded retainer. Also for executives who don't need a system built — they just need the thinking available.

**Price:** $650/month  
**Minimum Commitment:** 3 months

## 4. HOW THE INTAKE WIZARD ROUTES TO PRODUCTS

The Mission Intake Wizard's six-step process produces a JSON brief that maps directly to this product catalog. The routing logic below reflects the decision tree behind that mapping.

| Intake Track Selection | Primary Recommendation | Secondary Option | Consultation First? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Research OS | Signal Stack (Pilot Build) | Competitive Watch Room | Yes, if AI comfort is "new" |
| Content Engine | Repurposing Machine (Pilot Build) | Brand Voice Archive | Yes, if no content system exists |
| Local Business Ops | The Front Door (Pilot Build) | Delivery Rail | Yes, if workflow is informal |
| Executive Assistant OS | Weekly Brief System (Pilot Build) | Decision Architecture | Situational |
| Technical Advisory | System Design (consult) | AI Readiness Assessment | Always |
| Not Sure Yet | The Situation Map (consult) | The Orientation (Tier I) | Yes — always |

---

### Routing Decision Logic

**"Not Sure Yet"**
→ Book The Orientation (Tier I). The Situation Map comes out of that session. No build is scoped until the map exists.

**"Research OS" + AI Comfort = New/Beginner**
→ The Orientation first, then Signal Stack Pilot Build. Skip competitive watch until the daily brief habit is formed.

**"Research OS" + AI Comfort = Moderate/Advanced**
→ Signal Stack or Competitive Watch Room as a direct Pilot Build. System Design available if they have internal resources.

**"Content Engine" + small solo creator**
→ Brand Voice Archive first (1 week, low commitment), then Repurposing Machine. The voice needs to be on paper before the machine runs it.

**"Content Engine" + team of 3+**
→ Repurposing Machine + Publishing Rail as a bundle. The team coordination problem is usually the bigger issue.

**"Local Business Ops" + lead intake is the primary pain**
→ The Front Door, directly into Pilot Build.

**"Local Business Ops" + delivery/project management is the primary pain**
→ Delivery Rail, directly into Pilot Build.

**"Executive Assistant OS" + solo executive**
→ Weekly Brief System. Decision Architecture can come later.

**"Executive Assistant OS" + team of 10+**
→ Consider Tier III (Operating System) — the executive's needs usually reveal cross-functional system requirements.

**"Technical Advisory"**
→ AI Readiness Assessment or System Design. These clients often want to implement themselves — give them the architecture.

**Budget flag in intake: under $3,000**
→ Route to The Orientation first. Set expectations. Then The Front Door or Signal Stack depending on track.

**Budget flag in intake: $10,000+**
→ Consider whether the scale warrants an Operating System scoping conversation rather than a Pilot Build.

**Timeline: "ASAP / under 4 weeks"**
→ Pilot Build only. Be explicit that OS builds require 8+ weeks.

**Timeline: "No rush / 6+ months"**
→ Full System Design available. Can scope an Operating System with proper runway.
